# About Hope Spot Organization (HSO)

This document is the canonical organizational reference for AI agents and team members. Use it as the source of truth when communication requires factual context about who HSO is, what we do, and how we work.

## Summary

Hope Spot Organization (HSO) is a Norwegian humanitarian non-governmental organization based in Trondheim. We help refugees and immigrants integrate into Norwegian society through practical support — language classes, job training, guidance, counseling, and community connection. We work with people from many backgrounds, with significant communities including Arabic speakers and Ukrainian refugees.

## Mission

HSO helps refugees and immigrants integrate into their new societies in Norway by providing:

- Language classes (Norwegian and others as needed)
- Job training and employment guidance
- Counseling and personal support
- Community-building activities and cultural events
- General guidance for navigating Norwegian systems

## Who We Serve

People who have come to Norway as refugees or immigrants, regardless of background. The communities we currently work with most actively include:

- Arabic-speaking refugees (many backgrounds — Syrian, Iraqi, Palestinian, others)
- Ukrainian refugees who arrived following the 2022 invasion
- Other immigrant communities in the Trondheim region

We do not require religious affiliation, citizenship status, or any other qualification to access our services. We are a non-religious organization that respects all faiths and backgrounds.

## Our Approach

We work *alongside* the people we serve, not above them. Several principles guide how we work:

**Practical first.** We focus on what helps right now — language to find a job, knowledge to navigate paperwork, connections to feel less alone.

**Dignity always.** People come to us already adults with skills, history, and resilience. We don't infantilize, save, or pity. We support.

**Community-rooted.** Our events bring people together across backgrounds. We are part of the Trondheim community, not separate from it.

**Multilingual by design.** We meet people in their own language whenever we can.

**No religious agenda.** We are not faith-based. We respect that many of our community members have strong faith traditions, and our cultural events (like Iftar) honor them — but we are not a religious organization.

## Volunteers

Volunteers are central to our work. Our volunteer base includes Norwegians and settled immigrants who give their time to teach language, support job search, organize events, mentor newcomers, and run programs.

We hold regular volunteer orientations, an annual general meeting, and celebrations to recognize volunteer contributions. Anyone interested in volunteering can reach out via the website or come to our office.

## What We're Not

To prevent misunderstandings, here's what HSO is *not*:

- Not a legal services provider — we can guide and refer, but we don't represent people in immigration cases
- Not a faith-based organization
- Not a government agency or quasi-government
- Not a refugee resettlement agency in the formal sense — we support people who are already here
- Not a housing provider
- Not a medical provider
