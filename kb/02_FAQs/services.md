# Services FAQ

This document covers the most common questions about HSO's services. The email agent uses it to answer service-related enquiries.

## Summary

HSO offers free services to refugees and immigrants in Trondheim — Norwegian language classes, job training, counseling and guidance, and community events. Most services are open to anyone without registration; some require basic sign-up.

## What HSO Is Not

For clarity in agent responses, HSO is not:

- A legal services provider (we can refer)
- A medical or mental health clinic (we can refer)
- A government agency
- A housing provider
- A religious organization

If someone asks about services we don't provide, we acknowledge what they need and refer them to better-suited resources.

## Edge Cases and Escalation

If someone's enquiry falls outside the patterns above, the agent should:

- For unclear questions: ask for clarification or invite them to visit/call
- For sensitive personal situations: acknowledge with care and escalate to a human
- For specific case questions ("can you help with my asylum application?"): explain that we don't provide legal services and offer referrals
- For urgent situations: escalate immediately

## Examples of Agent Responses

### Example 1: Basic class enquiry

**Email:** "Hi, I just arrived in Trondheim. Where can I learn Norwegian?"

**Response approach:**
- Welcome them
- Confirm classes are free and explain what's offered
- Give the address and (verified) schedule
- Invite them to drop by
- Mention that they don't need to register first

### Example 2: Specific question we don't know the answer to

**Email:** "What time does the intermediate Norwegian class start on Thursday?"

**Response approach:**
- If we have the schedule confirmed in this document, give it
- If we don't, give general guidance and invite them to call or drop by to confirm
- Never invent specific times

### Example 3: Question about something we don't offer

**Email:** "Can you help me with my asylum interview next week?"

**Response approach:**
- Acknowledge the importance of what they're going through
- Be clear that we don't provide legal services for asylum cases
- Refer to qualified legal services (NOAS, etc.)
- Offer that they're welcome to come by anyway if other support would help
