# Volunteering FAQ

This is a starter draft. HSO leadership must verify and complete this content before agent use.

## Summary

HSO has an active volunteer program. Volunteers help with language classes, conversation groups, mentoring, event support, translation, and general program assistance.

## How do I become a volunteer?

The simplest way: come to one of our orientations, or send us an email at Hope.spot.org@gmail.com.

## Do I need specific qualifications?

No. We welcome volunteers from many backgrounds. Some roles benefit from specific skills (teaching experience, language fluency), but most need only willingness to show up and engage warmly.

## I have specific professional skills (legal, medical, accounting, etc.) — can I help with those?

If a volunteer mentions specific professional skills, the agent should:
- Thank them warmly
- Acknowledge that those skills could be very valuable
- Note that we'll have someone follow up specifically about how to use those skills well
- Flag for human follow-up

We don't want to commit to specific use of professional skills via the agent — those need real conversations.

## What if I want to stop volunteering?

We're grateful for any time you give. There's no obligation to continue. Just let us know — we'd love to thank you properly.

[Note: any "volunteer wants to stop" messages should escalate to a human, not be auto-handled.]

## Edge cases to escalate

- Specific professional skills offered (legal, medical, etc.)
- Volunteers asking for reference letters or formal documentation
- Volunteers reporting safety concerns
- Volunteers reporting interpersonal conflicts
- Anyone wanting to volunteer with vulnerable populations (minors, women in domestic violence situations, etc.) — these need vetting
